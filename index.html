May 15, 2025 • Switzerland

Hiking the Swiss Alps

My journey through the Swiss Alps was nothing short of magical. Starting in the picturesque village of Grindelwald, I embarked on a 5-day trek that would take me through some of the most stunning landscapes I've ever witnessed.

The first day was challenging with a steep ascent to the Faulhorn summit at 2,681 meters. The reward? A breathtaking panoramic view of the surrounding peaks and lakes that made every step worth it. I stayed overnight at the historic Faulhorn mountain hut, built in 1830, which offered cozy accommodations and delicious Swiss cheese fondue.

The following days brought me through flower-filled alpine meadows, past crystal-clear mountain lakes, and across thrilling suspension bridges. The highlight was reaching the Schynige Platte ridge with its famous view of the Eiger, Mönch, and Jungfrau mountains.

Travel Tips

  • Best time to visit: June to September for hiking
  • Essential gear: Good hiking boots, layers for changing weather, and trekking poles
  • Mountain huts book up quickly - reserve in advance
  • Swiss Travel Pass can save money on transportation
  • Try the local specialties: cheese fondue and rösti
April 22, 2025 • Bali, Indonesia

Paradise Found in Bali

Bali exceeded all my expectations with its perfect blend of natural beauty, rich culture, and warm hospitality. I spent two weeks exploring this Indonesian paradise, from its famous beaches to hidden waterfalls in the jungle.

My journey began in Ubud, the cultural heart of Bali. Here I visited the Sacred Monkey Forest, explored ancient temples like Goa Gajah, and took a traditional Balinese cooking class. The highlight was waking up at 2am to hike Mount Batur for sunrise - the view over the caldera lake was unforgettable.

The second week took me to Bali's stunning coastline. I discovered secret beaches in Uluwatu, surfed gentle waves in Canggu, and snorkeled with manta rays in Nusa Penida. The most magical moment was watching the sunset at Tanah Lot temple, perched dramatically on a rock in the sea.

Travel Tips

  • Visa: Many nationalities get 30-day visa on arrival
  • Best areas: Ubud for culture, Uluwatu for beaches, Canggu for digital nomads
  • Transport: Rent a scooter or use Grab (Southeast Asia's Uber)
  • Must-try foods: Nasi goreng, babi guling, and fresh coconut
  • Respect local customs: Dress modestly at temples
March 8, 2025 • Kyoto, Japan

Temples of Kyoto

Kyoto, Japan's ancient capital, is home to over 1,600 Buddhist temples and 400 Shinto shrines. My week-long pilgrimage through these sacred sites was a journey through time and spirituality.

I began at Kinkaku-ji, the Golden Pavilion, whose top two floors are completely covered in gold leaf. The reflection of the temple in the mirror pond was mesmerizing. Next was Ryoan-ji with its famous Zen rock garden, where I sat in meditation contemplating the 15 carefully placed rocks (one is always hidden from view).

The highlight was Fushimi Inari-taisha with its thousands of vermilion torii gates winding up the mountain. I hiked the entire 4km trail to the summit at dawn, passing through this seemingly endless tunnel of gates. At the top, I was rewarded with panoramic views of Kyoto and a profound sense of peace.

Travel Tips

  • Best seasons: Spring for cherry blossoms or autumn for fall colors
  • Get a Kyoto City Bus Pass for unlimited temple hopping
  • Visit popular temples early morning to avoid crowds
  • Try shojin ryori (Buddhist vegetarian cuisine)
  • Respect temple etiquette: remove shoes, no photos where prohibited
February 14, 2025 • Tanzania

African Safari Adventure

My 10-day safari through Tanzania's Northern Circuit was the wildlife experience of a lifetime. From the vast plains of the Serengeti to the Ngorongoro Crater, I witnessed nature's most spectacular shows.

The adventure began in Tarangire National Park, famous for its massive elephant herds and ancient baobab trees. Watching a family of elephants interact at a watering hole, I was struck by their intelligence and social bonds. Next was Lake Manyara, where I saw tree-climbing lions lounging in acacia trees like house cats.

The highlight was the Serengeti during the Great Migration. I witnessed thousands of wildebeest and zebra crossing crocodile-infested rivers, with predators lurking nearby. The raw drama of life and death unfolding before my eyes was both thrilling and humbling. The trip concluded in Ngorongoro Crater, a natural amphitheater teeming with wildlife including the rare black rhino.

Travel Tips

  • Best time: Dry season (June-October) for wildlife viewing
  • Pack neutral-colored clothing (no bright colors or white)
  • Bring binoculars and a good zoom lens for photography
  • Choose ethical operators that support conservation
  • Malaria prophylaxis is essential - consult a doctor
